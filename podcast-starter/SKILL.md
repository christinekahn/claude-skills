# Podcast Starter Skill

A complete production and marketing workflow for an interview-style podcast focused on
professional storytelling. Covers pre-production → episode publishing → two-week social campaign.

Read `references/brand-voice.md` before writing any copy. Brand identity is the foundation
of everything this skill produces.

---

## Workflow Overview

The skill supports five distinct deliverables. Identify which one(s) the user needs
and jump directly to that section:

1. **Guest Prep Doc** — warm briefing sent to the guest before recording
2. **Host Script** — intro/outro + question arc for the host
3. **Episode Description & Show Notes** — Buzzsprout-ready copy
4. **Two-Week LinkedIn Rhythm** — 5-post sequence per episode drop
5. **Social Variants** — Substack essay, Instagram caption, Threads post

If the user says "make everything for EP05" or similar, produce all five in one pass.

---

## 1. Guest Prep Doc

**Purpose:** Help the guest arrive feeling grounded and warm, not over-prepared.
The doc is a preview of the conversation arc, not a script.

**Format:**

```
Hi [Guest First Name],

[1–2 sentences: genuine personal connection or shared history — why THIS guest on THIS show]

Here's a look at what we'll cover so you can come in feeling grounded.
The recording will be about 30 minutes. Nothing formal, just a conversation
where you share your story. I'll record via Riverside.fm.

[Episode number and title]: [Subtitle / thematic framing]

[2–3 sentence bio paragraph exactly matching this format:]
[Guest Name] is [current role] at [company] and [second role if applicable].
[One sentence on career arc or signature work.] [One sentence on the personal/creative
dimension that connects to the show theme.]

This is a conversation about [2–3 specific themes in plain language].

The Arc
- [Question or prompt — casual, not formal]
- [Question or prompt]

[Section Header — thematic, not generic]
- [Question]
- [Question]
- [Question]

[Section Header]
- [Question]

Closing
- [Closing prompt about lessons or advice]
- Outside of work — what are you building, creating, or chasing right now?
  [Brief personalized framing of why this question lands for this guest]

Let me know if anything feels off or if you'd like to add/skip something. Can't wait!

Christine
```

**Rules:**
- Always end with the signature closing question, personalized to the guest
- Keep question count to 12–16 total across all sections
- Tone: warm, peer-to-peer, unhurried — never formal or exhaustive
- Do NOT say "Zoom" — recording tool is **Riverside.fm**
- Guest bio: career arc → current work, 3 sentences max, no credential list
- Reference specific personal details (shared history, creative project, a story the guest has told)

## 2. Host Script

**Purpose:** Give Christine a tight, confident intro and outro.
The middle is conversation — she does not need a script for questions.

**Format:**

```
[INTRO — ~90 seconds when read aloud]

Welcome to Dreams and Delivery, a podcast for tech professionals —
real stories from people who deliver at work and dream beyond it.
I'm your host, Christine Kahn. By day, I'm a staff technical program manager
in big tech. By night, I'm a musician and a sound bath meditation practitioner.

Today's guest is [warm descriptor — friend, colleague, collaborator].
[Guest Name] is [2-sentence arc: where they came from → what they built].
[One sentence: what this episode is really about, emotionally or thematically.]

[Guest first name], welcome.

---

[QUESTION ARC — abbreviated reminders, not full questions]
Opening: [topic]
Section 1 — [theme]: [2–3 bullet points]
Section 2 — [theme]: [2–3 bullet points]
Closing: [topic]
Signature: Outside of work — what are you building, creating, or chasing right now?

---

[OUTRO — ~45 seconds when read aloud]

[Guest first name], thank you so much. [One sentence: what the guest gave the listener —
specific, not generic.]

[One sentence: where listeners can find the guest — LinkedIn, website, Substack, etc.]

If this resonated, share it with someone in your life who's delivering at work
and dreaming of something more. You can find Dreams and Delivery on Spotify,
Apple Podcasts, and at dreamsanddelivery.buzzsprout.com.

Until next time — keep delivering, and keep dreaming.
```

## 3. Episode Description & Show Notes

**Purpose:** Buzzsprout-ready copy. Story leads, credentials follow.
This is what listeners read before pressing play.

**Format:**

```
[Episode Thematic Title — 3–5 words, evocative]

[Opening paragraph — 2–3 sentences. Lead with the story beat or tension,
not the guest's résumé. What did this person build, and what did it cost?]

[Second paragraph — 2–3 sentences. What this conversation is about.
Name 2–3 specific themes the listener will encounter.]

[Optional third paragraph — 1 sentence. The "bonus" or unexpected angle —
crochet, a friend in Paris, a sound bath, whatever the idiosyncratic detail is.]

About [Guest First Name] [Guest Last Name]

[3-sentence bio: current role → career arc → personal dimension.
Format: "[Name] is [role] at [company] and [second role if applicable].
[One sentence career arc.] [One sentence on what they build beyond work.]"]

Connect with [Guest First Name]: [LinkedIn URL] | [website or Substack if applicable]
```

**Rules:**
- Title: thematic, not descriptive ("The Frequency Shift" not "Mary-Lynne Williams on Sound Healing")
- First word of description is the guest's name or a story hook — never "In this episode"
- No bullet points in descriptions — prose only
- Host bio is footer only, never introduction

## 4. Two-Week LinkedIn Rhythm

Produce all 5 posts in one pass. Each post stands alone — no post should require
having read the others.

**Post 1 — Drop Day (publish day)**
Hook: story-first, emotionally resonant. Guest named mid-paragraph, not in the first line.
Format: 3–4 short paragraphs. End with: "Link in comments." + 3–4 hashtags.
Do NOT open with "Episode X" or the guest's name.

**Post 2 — Mid-week Quote Card (Day 3–4)**
One pull quote from the episode (12–25 words, punchy, self-contained).
Frame with 1 sentence of context above and 1 CTA sentence below.
End: "Link in comments."

**Post 3 — Personal Reflection (Day 5–6)**
Christine's 1st-person voice. What this conversation surfaced for her personally.
Connect to her dual identity (TPM + sound bath/musician) or her own story.
2–3 paragraphs. Intimate, not promotional.

**Post 4 — Skill/Insight Post (Week 2, Day 8–9)**
Extract one transferable skill or framework from the episode.
"Here's what I took away from [guest name]'s episode: …"
3–5 short paragraphs or a tight prose breakdown. Practical and actionable.

**Post 5 — Next Episode Tease (Day 12–13)**
1–2 paragraphs. No spoilers, just curiosity and momentum.
Connect thematically to the current episode if there's a natural arc.
End: "Dropping [day/date]. Subscribe so you don't miss it."

**Global LinkedIn rules:**
- Story hooks outperform episode-number leads
- Link always goes in comments, not the post body
- 3–4 hashtags max, lowercase, at the end
- Never open any post with "I'm excited to share" or "Check out"

## 5. Social Variants

**Substack Essay (~500–800 words)**
Angle: one idea from the episode, expanded. This is NOT a recap.
Open with a scene, question, or provocation.
Weave in 1–2 pull moments from the conversation.
End with an invitation to listen or a reflection question for the reader.
Title: thematic, essay-style (not "EP03 Recap")

**Instagram Caption**
3–4 short paragraphs. Lead with an image-native hook ("This conversation stopped me.").
Middle: 2–3 specific things from the episode, not summaries.
End: "Link in bio." + 5–8 hashtags (mix of niche and broad).

**Threads Post**
2–3 short punchy paragraphs or a micro-thread of 3–4 standalone sentences.
Conversational register — like texting a smart friend.
End with one question to invite replies.

## Content Pillars (use to anchor all copy)

Every piece of content maps to one or more of these:

- **How We Work** — The craft of delivery: OCM, ops, program management
- **How We Deliver** — Execution stories, transitions navigated, decisions under pressure
- **How We Think** — Frameworks, philosophy, mindset shifts
- **How We Dream** — What people build beyond their job titles

---

## Thematic Language (preserve across all copy)

Always available for use. Never force them — let them land naturally:

- *alignment* — are you aligned with who you're becoming?
- *frequency / frequency shift* — resonant with the sound bath practice
- *vibration* — connects wellness identity with professional identity
- *nervous system* — grounds the wellness dimension in somatic language
- *dream beyond it* — the show's core invitation

---

## Season Arc Awareness

Episodes build on each other. When writing for EP04 or later, acknowledge
thematic connections to prior episodes when natural:

- EP01 (Jessica): career alignment, OCM, layoff-to-pivot
- EP02 (Mary-Lynne): frequency shift, sound healing, leaving tech
- EP03 (Martina): TPM skills as product, fractional leadership, working outside boxes
- EP04 (Aaron): dreaming with AI, embodied cognition, going independent
- EP05 (Nicole): creative ownership, Filipino cultural storytelling, building a catalog

EP04 → EP05 arc: AI/abstract → grounded/practical creative ownership.
